About me:

Student at the Department of Informatics, University of Rijeka with a passion for learning and problem solving.

Optimistic, straightforward and a perfectionist at heart.

I started programming when I was 11 years old in 6th grade, primary school. Worked in QBasic and went to a couple of city, regional and one state competition. In middle school I mostly programmed in Visual Basic and some HTML as part of our Informatics class. In college I directed myself towards Web development. Started with PHP, then Python, taught myself jQuery and am still working on my JavaScript skills. Also HTML, CSS, and SQL are a part of what I learned and still use.

I also have some experience working with Java.

My main working machine has been running Ubuntu since 2010. I use NetBeans, Eclipse, Spyder and the Linux terminal as my work environments.

I've participated in WhoHack #4 with my colleagues from Onetius where we won 4th place with the online game Barfight (not released). Also participated in Web Summit 2014. as an Alpha exhibitor with the Onetius project.

I've left Onetius due to professional differences and to focus on my own projects.

In 2014. I developed a data visualization script in R for a professor of the Department of Informatics who is working on a data mining project. In time I'd like to get into data mining as it looks interesting and useful.

I joined the Document Freedom Day 2015. event at the Department of Informatics where I held a presentation [in Croatian] titled "My experience with transition from MS Office 2007 to LibreOffice". I also talked about open document formats, mainly OOXML vs. ODF.

As mentioned, I'm currently bettering my JavaScript skills and working with (learning to use) MongoDB, Node and WebGL and teaching myself proper planning and documenting techniques while working on my Masters degree project.
